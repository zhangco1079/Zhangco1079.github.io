NPR News 03 15 2020 8PM ET

Support for this podcast and the following message come from Zoom. Zoom ties together communication tools into a single platform, including videoconferencing, phone calls and group chat, Zoom has more information online at zoom.com. Zoom, meet happy.

Live from NPR News in Washington, I’m Jeanine Herbst.

  1. Vice President Mike Pence says more Americans will have access to coronavirus tests in the coming days, with the roll-out of the new private-public partnership, NPR’s Alison Aubry has more.

    The head of the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases Anthony Fauci says the U.S. is moving into a new phase of testing. In addition to State Health Labs, commercial high-speed testing is being rolled out, making it possible to do thousands of tests. Officials say priority will be given to health care providers and older Americans at higher risk. So far there are about three thousand documented cases of coronavirus in the U.S., though this case count is expected to go up as testing increases. Fauci advises Americans hunker down to prevent the spread of this contagious virus. Alison Aubry, NPR News, Washington.
    Meanwhile, the CDC is recommending events with more than 50 people be canceled whether they were organized by groups or individuals. That recommendation though does not include schools and businesses.

  1. The Federal Reserve made a surprise move today, slashing interest rates to near 0, in an emergency action aimed at containing the economic damage of the coronavirus. But the move did little to sway investors as Wall Street futures were trading down by more than 4.5 percent tonight. Steve Buckner has more on the Fed’s actions.

    Less than two weeks after cutting the Federal funds rate half a percentage point, the Fed cut that key overnight money market rate another full percentage point. The Fed was scheduled to meet Wednesday, but citing risks to the economic outlook, it moved early to slash short-term interest rates to near 0 for the first time in more than 4 years. What’s more, the Fed reactivated so-called quantitative easing. It will buy at least 500 billion dollars of treasury securities and another 200 billion dollars of mortgage-backed securities to push down long-term rates. In cooperation with foreign central banks, it also took other actions to ease credit. For NPR News, I am Steve Buckner.

  1. The number of coronavirus cases in Italy has risen with 3600 new cases 368 deaths in just the last 24 hours. NPR’s Sylvia Poggioli reports

    The epicenter is in Lombardy in a cluster of small and medium-size towns with the outbreak surface on February 21. The government has imposed drastic restrictions on freedom of movement, in a bid to prevent the virus from spreading. Everything is being done to prevent a surge in cases in Milan, The Italian financial and economical locomotive and metropolis of some 3 million people. There’s a severe lack of ICU beds, ventilators, face masks, and other medical equipment. Lombardy welfare counselor Giulio Gallera said the number of cases is expected to rise for the next 14 days with the start of the decline at the end of next week. Sylvia Poggioli, NPR News, Rome. This is NPR.

  2. Senator Bernie Sanders and former Vice President Joe Biden are squaring off in the latest presidential debate being held at CNN’s studios in Washington without an audience, that because of the concerns over the coronavirus. It was supposed to have been held in Arizona. health care is expected to take center stage tonight as both have been very vocal on how they would handle the pandemic if they were in office. Sanders has been pitching his “Medicare for all” plan as Biden rides rather a wave of momentum, after scoring more than dozen wins in recent primaries.

  1. In Lebanon, the President has declared a medical emergency and encouraged people to work from home. NPR’s Alice Fordham reports security forces were deployed in the capital.

    Police in masks and gloves move people off the popular sea from the walkway in Beirut. Meanwhile, in neighbouring Syria, the government has not announced a single case of coronavirus, though credible rumors are circulating of many undeclared cases. Officials from the International Red Cross recently visited Syria. One of them Fabrizio Carboni said coronavirus would be a major challenge there.

    • The health system in Syria was obviously dramatically affected by the conflicts, you had a lot of health infrastructure destroyed, you had a lot of health professionals who left this country. Alice Fordham, NPR News, Beirut.

  2. U.S. futures contracts are trading lower at this hour, all three major indices are down more than 4.5 percent, The Asia Dow was down a fraction. I’m Jeanine Herbst, NPR News in Washington.
